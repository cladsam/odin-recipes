Nitamago (Soft boiled egg)

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Instructions

  1. Fill a pot with water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Gently place the eggs into the boiling water and cook for 6-7 minutes for a soft-boiled consistency.
  3. While the eggs are cooking, prepare an ice bath by filling a large bowl with cold water and ice cubes.
  4. Once the eggs are done, carefully transfer them to the ice bath to cool down and stop the cooking process.
  5. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, water, and sugar.
    Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is dissolved.
  6. Once the eggs have cooled, carefully peel them and place them in a ziplock bag or a container.
  7. Pour the marinade mixture over the eggs, ensuring they are fully submerged.
    Remove any excess air from the bag or cover the container tightly.
  8. Marinate the eggs in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse.
  9. After marinating, remove the eggs from the marinade and slice them in half.
    The Nitamago eggs are now ready to be served as a delicious topping for your ramen or enjoyed as a snack.

Specificity

Nitamago is a marinated soft-boiled egg commonly used as a topping in ramen.
It features a creamy yolk and tender white, adding richness and flavor to the dish.
